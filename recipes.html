Fettuccine Alfredo

This classic Italian pasta dish includes fettuccine tossed with butter and Parmesan cheese, and it's quick and easy to make. U.S. versions of the dish may include chicken or shrimp.

Ingredients

  • Fettuccine pasta
  • Freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • Butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • (Optional) Grilled chicken or shrimp

Instructions

Cook the fettuccine according to package instructions. In a separate pan, melt the butter and add the cooked pasta. Toss with Parmesan cheese until the pasta is coated. Season with salt and pepper. Serve hot.

Chimichurri

Chimichurri is a sauce used for grilled meat, originally from Argentina. It's made from parsley, garlic, oil, oregano, and white vinegar.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup fresh flat-leaf parsley
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • 2 tablespoons fresh oregano leaves
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons white wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions

Finely chop the parsley, garlic, and oregano. Combine in a bowl with olive oil, vinegar, salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes if using. Let it sit for at least 20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

French Omelette

A French omelette is a classic and versatile breakfast dish. Unlike a denser American omelette, it's cooked very quickly in a super-hot pan and often rolled or folded to encase a filling.

Ingredients

  • 3 eggs
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh herbs (optional)
  • Cheese (optional)

Instructions

Beat the eggs with salt, pepper, and herbs if using. Heat butter in a non-stick skillet over medium-high heat. Pour in the eggs and cook for a few seconds, until the bottom starts to set. Using a spatula, gently fold the omelette in half or roll it. Serve immediately.

Classic Margherita Pizza

A simple yet classic pizza, topped with fresh tomatoes, mozzarella cheese, fresh basil, salt, and extra-virgin olive oil.

Ingredients

  • Pizza dough (homemade or store-bought)
  • 2-3 ripe tomatoes, thinly sliced
  • 200g fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra-virgin olive oil
  • Salt

Instructions

Preheat your oven to the highest setting. Roll out the pizza dough and place it on a baking tray. Arrange the tomato slices and mozzarella cheese on top. Drizzle with olive oil and a pinch of salt. Bake for 10-15 minutes until the crust is golden and cheese is bubbly. Garnish with fresh basil leaves before serving.

Gnocchi Quattro Formaggi

This dish features soft gnocchi pasta covered in a creamy four-cheese blend, offering a rich and indulgent flavor.

Ingredients

  • 500g potato gnocchi
  • 50g Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 50g Gorgonzola cheese, crumbled
  • 50g Fontina cheese, cubed
  • 50g Mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

In a pot, bring salted water to a boil and cook gnocchi according to package instructions. In a separate pan, melt butter over medium heat, add heavy cream and all the cheeses. Stir until the cheeses melt and the sauce becomes creamy. Season with salt and pepper. Drain the gnocchi and add them to the sauce, gently tossing to coat. Serve immediately.
